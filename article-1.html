Magnus Carlsen Dominates at World Chess Championship

Magnus Carlsen has successfully defended his title as FIDE World Chess Champion, scoring an impressive fourth win over Ian Nepomniachtchi after just eleven rounds.

The contest, held in Dubai, was won by Carlsen with 7.5 points to 3.5, a margin matched only by Capablanca’s victory over Lasker 100 years ago. Initially appearing bound for a draw, the match quickly came undone after Nepomniachtchi made a series of uncharacteristic blunders.

The match also contained the longest ever World Championship game ever played, totaling a mind-boggling 136 moves lasting 7 hours and 45 minutes, with Carlsen squeezing out a hard-fought win in the endgame.

“It’s hard to feel that great joy when the situation was so comfortable to begin with, but I’m happy with a very good performance overall,” said Carlsen after securing the title.
